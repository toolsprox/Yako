## The Island of Spices

Sri Lanka has been globally renowned as the "Spice Island" since the days of the Silk Road. Its strategic location in the Indian Ocean, combined with a perfectly humid, tropical climate, produced spices so exceptional that empires went to war over them. 

The Dutch, Portuguese, and British all sought control of the island primarily for its lucrative spice trade, particularly for one crown jewel: **True Cinnamon (*Cinnamomum verum*)**.

Unlike the sharper *Cassia* cinnamon found in most Western supermarkets, True Sri Lankan Cinnamon is delicate, sweet, and highly aromatic. It forms the backbone of almost every traditional dish we serve.

## The Roasted Curry Powder (Badapu Thuna Paha)

The defining characteristic that separates Sri Lankan cuisine from its Indian neighbors is the use of *Badapu Thuna Paha* — the heavily roasted Sri Lankan curry powder. 

While Northern Indian curries often rely on lighter, aromatic blends, Southern Sri Lankan meat curries use a blend of spices that are dry-roasted in a pan until they are dark brown, almost black. This roasting process releases deep, smoky, and complex essential oils.

A typical Sri Lankan roasted curry powder contains:
* Coriander seeds
* Cumin seeds
* Fennel seeds
* Black Peppercorns
* Mustard seeds
* Cloves
* Cardamom pods
* True Cinnamon
* Curry leaves & Pandan (Rampe)

## Sourcing for Authenticity in London

When we opened Yako London, we made a commitment to absolute authenticity. You cannot recreate the true taste of a Sri Lankan Black Pork Curry or a Jaffna Crab Curry using generic supermarket spices. 

This is why our spices are sourced directly from independent farmers in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. They are sun-dried, roasted in small batches, and flown to our kitchen in Pinner. 

Every time you smell the intoxicating aroma of cardamom, roasted coriander, and toasted coconut drifting from our kitchen, you are experiencing the authentic, uncompromised scent of the Spice Island.
